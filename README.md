I am a Ph.D. student at the University of Tsukuba, working on wearable robotics, human motor assistance, and rehabilitation-oriented control systems, advised by [Dr. Kawamoto Hiroaki](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=_WhZt68AAAAJ&hl=en), [Dr. Akira Uehara](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=44wfZqUAAAAJ&hl=ja) and [Dr Yoshiyuki Sankai](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=zuk28pEAAAAJ&hl=en).  I belong to [Cybernics laboratory](https://sanlab.iit.tsukuba.ac.jp/)

My research focuses on wearable robotic systems that support postural control and lateral weight shifting during walking. I am particularly interested in assistive technologies for people with motor impairments, including Parkinsonian gait disturbances, and in control strategies that respond to human movement intention.

My work lies at the intersection of wearable robotics, biomechanics, rehabilitation science, and control engineering. I aim to develop human-centered robotic systems that improve mobility, stability, and daily quality of life.

I'm also working on a humanoid robot project and [non-taouch vital sensing project](https://sanlab.iit.tsukuba.ac.jp/monitoring-system/) as a research assistant.
